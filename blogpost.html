AI dangerous for children?

Artificial intelligence can be useful and potentially risky for children. On the one hand, artificial intelligence can provide valuable educational resources, help with homework, and promote creativity through interactive tools and games. It can also help children with disabilities and those with special needs. However, there are also risks associated with artificial intelligence that parents should be aware of. For example, if children are exposed to biased or harmful content, it can negatively affect their social and emotional development. In addition, there is a risk of addiction to AI-powered devices or online platforms, which can lead to negative effects on mental health and general well-being. It is important for parents to monitor their children's use of artificial intelligence and ensure that they use it in a safe and responsible manner. This includes setting limits on screen time, monitoring harmful content, and encouraging a healthy balance between online and offline activities.
